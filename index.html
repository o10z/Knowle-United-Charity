About Knowle United Charity

Knowle United Charity traces its origins back to the 17th Century and over the last 300 years the Charity has been providing benefits to people in the ancient ecclesiastical parishes of St. John the Baptist, Knowle; St. Philip’s, Dorridge; St. Swithin’s, Barston and the civil parishes of Balsall Common & Chadwick End. The Charity’s governing document, which is registered with the Charity Commission, specifies that any grants made by the Charity are limited to benefit residents within these areas.

Knowle United Charity owns 22 properties of various types and sizes in the village of Knowle and these are rented to people of limited means who have a close connection to Knowle at the time of their application. The Charity does not retain a waiting list and vacancies are advertised locally when any property becomes available. There is no warden or other support provided by the Charity.

Apply for a Grant

The Charity is able to consider applications for a small grant to assist eligible residents in the relief of need and/or the promotion of educational purposes.
